# MyEnergy Usability Validation Plan

Stage: 6  
Scope: moderated usability validation of the legacy and redesigned MyEnergy portal, with comparison support for classroom demonstration.

This plan is suitable for a professional moderated study. It does not assume prior user-test findings and does not claim results in advance.

## 1. Research Objectives

- Validate whether the redesigned portal improves task recognition, orientation, and confidence.
- Check whether the main customer tasks can be completed with fewer unnecessary decisions and less memory burden.
- Identify remaining risks in language, feedback, mobile layout, and support routing.
- Compare legacy and redesigned behavior without treating the redesign as automatically successful.

## 2. Research Questions

- Can participants identify the right path for each core task more quickly in the redesigned portal?
- Do participants need to remember fewer values while completing meter, payment, and tariff flows?
- Does the redesigned portal provide clearer feedback and a stronger sense of completion?
- Do the redesigned task labels match participant expectations and vocabulary?
- Where do participants still hesitate, backtrack, or ask for help?

## 3. Participant Profile

- Adults who have used a utility, billing, or customer self-service portal.
- Mix of first-time, returning, and digitally less-confident users.
- Comfortable reading plain web content in English.
- Able to use desktop or mobile browsers.

## 4. Recommended Participant Mix

- 2 first-time or infrequent portal users
- 2 returning portal users
- 2 digitally less-confident users
- 2 desktop-first users
- 2 mobile-first users
- 1-2 keyboard-focused users

Recommended total: 8-10 participants.

## 5. Session Format

- 60 minutes per session
- Moderated remote or in-person
- Single participant per session
- Screen and audio recording with consent
- Legacy and redesigned task execution plus comparison discussion
- Short wrap-up interview at the end

## 6. Introduction Script

> Thanks for joining. We are testing the portal, not you. I may ask you to think aloud while completing a few tasks. There are no trick questions, and you can stop at any time. We want to understand how the interface works, where it creates effort, and what feels clear or unclear.

## 7. Neutral Moderator Guidance

- Do not coach participants toward the correct answer.
- Avoid confirming success until the participant finishes a task.
- Use prompts such as “What are you looking for?” and “What do you expect to happen next?”
- If the participant is stuck, ask where they would look next rather than explaining the solution.
- Keep tone neutral and avoid design judgment during the session.

## 8. Five Task Scenarios

### Latest invoice

You need a copy of your most recent electricity bill for your records. Use the portal to obtain it.

### Meter reading

You want to submit your current meter reading so your billing stays up to date. Enter it in the portal.

### Monthly payment

Your budget has changed and you want to adjust your monthly payment. Use the portal to review the current amount and make a change.

### Tariff comparison

You are considering whether a different tariff would suit you better. Compare your current option with alternatives and, if appropriate, start the change process.

### Customer support

You have a question that you cannot solve on your own. Use the portal to contact support and explain your issue.

## 9. Observation Criteria

- First-click choice
- Time to locate the relevant task entry point
- Whether the participant uses task language or guesses from internal labels
- Number of backtracks or repeated route changes
- Whether the participant remembers a value from an earlier screen
- Whether the participant notices feedback or confirmation
- Whether the participant asks for help
- Whether the participant appears confident at completion

## 10. Post-Task Questions

- What made that task easy or difficult?
- What did you expect would happen when you selected that option?
- Did you feel certain that the task completed successfully?
- Was any wording confusing or unfamiliar?
- What would you change to make that task easier?

## 11. Overall Interview Questions

- Which portal felt clearer overall, and why?
- Where did you feel the most uncertainty?
- Did the task labels match how you think about these jobs?
- Which parts felt efficient, and which felt slow?
- What would make you trust the portal more?

## 12. Success Metrics

Behavioral measures:

- Task completion
- Completion without assistance
- Time on task
- Wrong turns
- Backtracking
- Error count
- Abandonment

Self-reported measures:

- Confidence rating
- Perceived effort
- Terminology comprehension
- Support required

Success criteria should be treated as hypotheses, not pre-existing facts:

- Fewer wrong turns on the redesigned portal than on the legacy portal
- Fewer memory-dependent recovery moments on meter, payment, and tariff tasks
- Higher confidence after completion on the redesigned portal
- Less moderator assistance needed for task entry and confirmation

## 13. Analysis Method

- Use task-by-task comparison across legacy and redesigned conditions.
- Note where a participant’s path differs from the intended path.
- Separate behavioral observations from participant comments.
- Tag observations against UX laws, error types, and terminology issues.
- Summarize evidence, inferences, and open questions separately.

## 14. Ethical and Privacy Considerations

- Obtain consent before recording.
- Explain how recordings and notes will be stored and who can access them.
- Do not collect personal account data.
- Use fictional account context only.
- Allow participants to stop or skip a task at any time.
- Avoid collecting sensitive personal information unless it is directly relevant to the study and explicitly consented to.

## 15. Limitations

- This study measures interface behavior, not business outcomes.
- Results will depend on the participant mix and study setting.
- A moderated session can identify friction, but it does not replace production analytics.
- Because the portal is fictional, the study can validate task design and comprehension, not real billing-system behavior.
